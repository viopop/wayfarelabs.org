About

Wayfare Labs began as a question posed by Erwin McManus at the 2013 Q gathering in Los Angeles:

What if the church was the human incubator for the world’s best future?

This question became the focal point of conversations for cofounders Brandon Arbini and Jeff Tanner throughout that week and beyond, resulting in a vision to form a community of entrepreneurs compelled by their faith to bring forth vital, dynamic human organizations that help heal our broken world.

The result–a coworking community + event space called Wayfare and a startup incubator for restorative movements called Wayfare Labs.

Wayfare Labs, organized as a California non-profit corporation, runs a startup incubator program for founders seeking to birth organizations that intentionally pursue the world’s best future.

These organizations may be formed as for-profit, non-profit, or hybrid entities. Wayfare Labs is available to act as fiscal sponsor for organizations forming as non-profits prior to their receipt of 501(c)(3) status, accelerating their launch through the ability to receive tax deductible donations.

Program Details

The first four months of the incubator program are intensive, where the newly forming organizations are led to develop their story, draft their business model, introduce a pilot, and pitch their vision to prospective partners.

The following eight months (and more if desired) provides them with continued Wayfare membership and access to coaching, workshops, resources, tools, networks, and emotional support to build their organization into viability.

We’re on the hunt for those interested in founding new organizations and those passionate about providing resources and support for their work.

FAQ

  1. What happens at Wayfare Labs?

    Wayfare Labs, a California non-profit corporation, runs a one-year startup incubator program for founders seeking to birth organizations that intentionally pursue the world’s best future.

  2. How much time per week should I expect?

    Startups require full-time commitment. Through Wayfare’s unlimited membership package, you’ll have full access to work out of the Wayfare office to develop your organization. Participation in the program events–dinners, workshops, and presentations will make up a small portion of your full time work week.

  3. What happens at the dinners?

    You will get time to share your experiences with fellow cohort members over a meal with one another. There will also be off the record talks with people that have gone through the process of launching a startup, which will include Q & A.

  4. What happens at the presentations nights?

    Presentation nights will consist of the cohort members developing a formal presentation encompassing the elements being learned through the program. This presentation will build upon the previous presentation as new topics are introduced in the workshops. At the close of the 4 month program, the presentations will be formally pitched to potential donors.

  5. What happens at the workshops?

    Workshops are meant to serve as a launching platform for new areas of exploration for your startup. They will guide your thinking to further develop the story, business model, pilot, and ultimately the launch of your start up.

  6. Do we need to have a business plan before we begin the program?

    Nope! No matter where you are at in the planning process for your organization, we will walk alongside of you to work toward your goals.

  7. Can we be a part of the program without moving to where you are?

    A critical part of our mission is that founders going through our program are immersed in a community and encouraging and supporting one another. With that said, it would be required that participants are able to work from our offices located in Costa Mesa, CA.

  8. How much does it cost?

    Program fees are $2,000/month for the first four months and $1,000/month after. Collaborative donor development tools and campaigns will be provided to assist in raising these program fees as well as additional funds for business expenses.

  9. How can we get funding for our startup?

    We will help you with resources and ideas about methods of fundraising and then the rest is up to you! Fundraising and networking with potential donors is a skill needed for any organization to be successful, so we believe this will be a great opportunity to practice.
