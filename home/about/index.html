Wayfare Labs began as a question posed by Erwin McManus at the 2013 Q gathering in Los Angeles:

What if the church was the human incubator for the world’s best future?

This question became the focal point of conversations for cofounders Brandon Arbini and Jeff Tanner throughout that week and beyond, resulting in a vision to form a community of entrepreneurs compelled by their faith to bring forth vital, dynamic human organizations that help heal our broken world.

The result–a coworking community + event space called Wayfare and a startup incubator for restorative movements called Wayfare Labs.

Wayfare Labs, organized as a California non-profit corporation, runs a startup incubator program for founders seeking to birth organizations that intentionally pursue the world’s best future.

These organizations may be formed as for-profit, non-profit, or hybrid entities. Wayfare Labs is available to act as fiscal sponsor for organizations forming as non-profits prior to their receipt of 501(c)(3) status, accelerating their launch through the ability to receive tax deductible donations.

Program Details

The first four months of the incubator program are intensive, where the newly forming organizations are led to develop their story, draft their business model, introduce a pilot, and pitch their vision to prospective partners.

The following eight months (and more if desired) provides them with continued Wayfare membership and access to coaching, workshops, resources, tools, networks, and emotional support to build their organization into viability.

We’re on the hunt for those interested in founding new organizations and those passionate about providing resources and support for their work.
